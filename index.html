Papers

On the Adversarial Robustness of Causal Algorithmic Recourse

Recourse recommendations should ideally be robust to reasonably small uncertainty in the features of the individual seeking recourse. We formulate the adversarially robust recourse problem, show that recourse methods offering minimally costly recourse fail to be robust, and present methods for generating adversarially robust recourse. In order to shift part of the burden of robustness from the individual to the decision-maker, we propose a model regularizer for training classifiers such that the additional cost of seeking robust recourse is reduced.

Ricardo Dominguez-Olmedo, Amir H. Karimi, Bernhard Schölkopf

Learning Temporally Coherent Policies for RL

Gaussian policies, ubiquitous in RL, produce action trajectories with low temporal coherence, thus resulting in inefficient exploration behaviours. We propose a recurrent policy which is regularized to encourage contiguous actions to be sufficiently correlated. For a simple continuous control environment, we show that our proposed policy achieves more effective exploration, improved sample efficiency and better asymptotic performance compared to Gaussian policies.

Ricardo Dominguez-Olmedo, Fabian Otto, Gerhard Neumann

report | presentation

Deep RL for Control of an Additive Manufacturing Process

Novel work on the use of deep reinforcement learning for the control of a complex additive manufacturing process. We model the dynamics of the thermomechanical process using a probabilistic ensemble of deep neural networks, and leverage model predictive control to achieve unprecedented control performance.

Ricardo Dominguez-Olmedo, George Panoutsos

Measuring cognitive workload from EEG brain signals

Researched on the use of EEG brain signals to measure cognitive workload, with the aim of evaluating if paralyzed patients could safely use a lower limb exoskeleton in their everyday life. Wrote software to automatically process the data, removing noise and muscular artifacts.
